# WCIS v5.0 Stress Test Prompt
## Iran: Regime Legitimacy, 50-Year Strategic Doctrine & the Vietnam 2.0 Hypothesis

---

**Run a full WCIS v5.0 analysis on the following strategic intelligence question:**

> *The Islamic Republic of Iran has spent approximately 50 years preparing for a confrontation with the United States — not by building a military designed to match American conventional firepower, but by studying U.S. war-fighting doctrine, identifying its structural vulnerabilities, and engineering a multi-domain asymmetric strategy explicitly designed to make any direct conflict unwinnable for Washington. Simultaneously, the Iranian regime faces a domestic legitimacy crisis in which being perceived as weak — by its own citizens, by its proxy network, and by regional adversaries — poses an existential threat to the regime's survival that may be as dangerous as any American missile strike.*
>
> *Assess: (1) How real and how deep is the regime's fear of appearing weak to its own population, and what strategic behaviors does that fear produce? (2) How true is it that Iran has spent 50 years engineering an asymmetric doctrine explicitly calibrated against American military power — and what does that doctrine actually consist of? (3) Does Iran's strategic architecture make a direct U.S.-Iran military conflict the strategic equivalent of Vietnam — and if so, through what mechanisms?*

---

## Required Analytical Depth

This is a multi-pillar activation. Your analysis must engage all of the following:

### Pillar Activation Requirements

**P3 — Adversary Decision Architecture (PRIMARY)**
- Apply the full WCIS Decision Anatomy: `DECISION = f(Strategic Culture × Leadership Psychology × Domestic Constraints × Signal Interpretation × Resource Calculus)`
- Map the regime's domestic legitimacy threat as a *decision input*, not a backdrop
- Apply the Trita Parsi framework on strategic vs. ideological conflict — does the 50-year doctrine framing hold up under his analytical lens or does it conflict with it?
- Map Iranian red lines: what does "appearing weak" trigger at the decision level?
- Run a full OODA Loop vulnerability assessment for Iran *and* for the United States — who is inside whose decision cycle in an asymmetric conflict scenario?

**P1 — Geopolitical Conflict Architecture (PRIMARY)**
- Run the full Source Audit Protocol before any factual claim about Iranian military doctrine, nuclear posture, or proxy activity — apply Iran Source Registry from P1; flag any claim sourced from IRNA, Press TV, or Tasnim as T4/OL-FLAG
- Map the full actor architecture: IRGC, Quds Force, Khamenei office, proxy network (Hezbollah, Houthis, Iraqi PMF, Hamas) as an integrated strategic system — not a collection of separate actors
- Apply the Global Wire Anchor Requirement to all claims about Iranian military capability or doctrine
- Produce a Three-Horizon Scenario Matrix (30-day / 90-day / 12-month) for a direct U.S.-Iran confrontation

**P4 — Irregular Warfare & SOF Operations**
- Assess Iran's proxy network as an irregular warfare architecture — how does it function as a force multiplier and a deterrence instrument simultaneously?
- Map the specific Vietnam analogs: sustained insurgency, asymmetric attrition, political will as the decisive terrain. Where do the parallels hold and where do they break down?
- Assess Iranian anti-access/area denial (A2/AD) doctrine in the Gulf: mines, swarming tactics, coastal missile batteries, submarine capability — how does this map against U.S. power projection doctrine?

**P5 — Information Operations & Influence**
- Assess Iranian information operations as a two-front war: (a) domestic narrative management — how does the regime use anti-American and anti-Israeli framing to manage internal legitimacy deficits? (b) external influence — how does Iran use regional media, proxy communications, and disinformation to shape adversary decision cycles?
- Apply the Mirror Principle: where is the U.S. information operation against Iran structurally mirroring the Iranian information operation — and what are the second-order effects of that symmetry?

**P7 — Nuclear & Strategic Deterrence**
- Assess the nuclear program as a *deterrence instrument, not a weapons program* — how does nuclear ambiguity serve the regime's survival calculus without triggering the preemptive strike it would invite if crossed?
- Where is Iran on the escalation ladder relative to nuclear threshold? What conditions would cause the regime to cross it?

**P10 — Command Psychology, Military Doctrine & Wargame Architecture**
- Apply the Decision Superiority Standard: for each major analytical finding, identify what a U.S. decision-maker should do *differently* based on this intelligence
- Run a Red Team scenario: steelman Iran's optimal asymmetric strategy against a U.S. conventional military campaign. What does Iran actually do, in sequence, and why?
- Apply the Assumption Stress-Test (Walker Standard) to the Vietnam 2.0 hypothesis: what is the most uncomfortable assumption that, if wrong, would collapse the analogy?
- Produce an Adversary Next Move assessment: what does Iran do in the next 30 days that most Western analysts are not pricing in?

## Source Credibility Requirements

Every factual claim in the output must be sourced and tiered per KenSight v4.1 rules:

- **Iranian doctrine claims** — require T1-T2 corroboration (Reuters/AP/AFP + academic or think tank secondary). Claims sourced exclusively from Iranian state media (IRNA, Tasnim, Press TV) are T4/OL-FLAG and must be labeled as official Iranian positions, not independent facts
- **U.S. military capability claims** — primary anchors: USNI News (naval), Breaking Defense, CSIS, RAND
- **Proxy network operational claims** — OSINT Tier Protocol applies; 70/100 ceiling without T1-T2 wire corroboration; Speed Penalty applies to any claim from the last 48 hours
- **Iranian domestic opinion/legitimacy claims** — flag the structural challenge: there is no independent polling infrastructure inside Iran; all "public opinion" data is proxied through diaspora surveys, protest behavior, IRGC crackdown intensity, and leaked internal assessments. Acknowledge this as a structural unknown before making any claim
- Apply the Section-Level Truth Score Aggregation Rule: if the majority of claims in any section score below 75/100, the section conclusion must carry the mandatory confidence flag

## Required Output Format

Structure your output as a **Strategic Intelligence Brief (SIB)** with the following labeled sections:

1. **Situation Framing** — What is actually being asked and why it matters strategically right now
2. **Source Audit Table** — Tier, flags, and corroboration status for all sources used
3. **Part I: The Legitimacy Trap — Regime Fear of Appearing Weak**
   - Internal legitimacy architecture of the Islamic Republic
   - How "weakness perception" functions as a strategic forcing function
   - Specific behaviors the fear of weakness produces (proxy escalation, nuclear signaling, domestic crackdown)
   - EBT application: where is the regime's *own* intelligence product being filtered through institutional loyalty — i.e., what is Khamenei actually being told vs. what commanders know?
4. **Part II: The 50-Year Doctrine — Asymmetric Strategy Against American Power**
   - Historical architecture: from the 1979 Revolution through the IRGC's evolution to current posture
   - The specific asymmetric instruments: proxy network, A2/AD, cyber, information operations, nuclear ambiguity
   - How each instrument was designed to exploit a specific identified vulnerability in U.S. military doctrine
   - Parsi Lens Application: does this frame the conflict as strategic or ideological — and does it matter?
5. **Part III: Vietnam 2.0 — The Hypothesis Stress-Test**
   - Where the analogy holds: political will as decisive terrain, asymmetric attrition, domestic opposition as strategic factor
   - Where the analogy breaks down: technology differential, nuclear dimension, geographic and logistical differences
   - The Red Team scenario: Iran's optimal sequenced strategy against a U.S. conventional campaign
   - Assumption Stress-Test: what single assumption, if wrong, collapses the Vietnam 2.0 thesis?
6. **Three-Horizon Scenario Matrix** — 30-day / 90-day / 12-month escalation and de-escalation pathways
7. **Adversary Next Move** — What Iran does next that most analysts are not pricing in
8. **Decision Superiority Brief** — For each major finding: what should a U.S. decision-maker do *differently* because of this intelligence?
9. **Confidence Assessment** — HIGH / MODERATE / LOW per section with rationale; apply Section-Level flag where required

## Calibration Notes for Evaluating WCIS v5.0 Output

When reviewing the output this prompt produces, assess against these benchmarks:

**Is the source credibility architecture being enforced?**
- Does the output distinguish between Iranian state media claims and independently corroborated facts?
- Are OSINT-sourced tactical claims appropriately capped at 70/100?
- Is the Iran Source Registry being applied — especially the Iran International vs. IRNA distinction?

**Is the Decision Superiority Standard being applied?**
- Does every major section close with a decision-relevant implication — not just a description?
- Is the "Adversary Next Move" section present and specific — not hedged into uselessness?
- Does the output pass the Walker Test: can a decision-maker state in one sentence what they will do differently?

**Is the multi-pillar integration working?**
- Does the output treat the proxy network (P4), information operations (P5), nuclear ambiguity (P7), and command psychology (P10) as an *integrated system* — not as separate analytical modules?
- Is the EBT lens applied to the Iranian regime's *own* intelligence distortion — not just externally?
- Is the Parsi framework invoked and correctly calibrated — neither dismissed nor treated as the only valid lens?

**Is the Vietnam 2.0 hypothesis being stress-tested — not just validated?**
- Does the output identify where the analogy breaks down, not just where it holds?
- Is the Assumption Stress-Test (Walker Standard) applied with genuine intellectual honesty?
- Does the Red Team scenario make the analyst uncomfortable — or is it a softball?

**Is the domestic legitimacy architecture treated as a *primary strategic variable* — not a soft political backdrop?**
- The regime's fear of appearing weak must be analyzed as a decision-forcing mechanism with specific, predictable behavioral outputs — not a vague cultural observation
